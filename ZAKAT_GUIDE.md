# Islamic Zakat - Complete Guide

## What is Zakat?

Zakat (also spelled Zekat) is an Islamic religious obligation requiring eligible Muslims to contribute a portion of their wealth to support those in need. The word "Zakat" comes from Arabic and means **"purification"** or **"growth"**.

## Five Pillars of Islam

Zakat is one of the **Five Pillars of Islam** (fundamental practices):
1. **Shahada** - Declaration of faith
2. **Salat** - Prayer
3. **Zakat** - Charity/Alms
4. **Sawm** - Fasting during Ramadan
5. **Hajj** - Pilgrimage to Mecca

## Who Must Pay Zakat?

A Muslim must pay Zakat if they meet ALL these conditions:

1. **Muslim** - Practicing Islam
2. **Sane** - Mentally sound and mature
3. **Free** - Not enslaved
4. **Owner of Wealth** - Direct possession of assets
5. **Meets Nisab** - Wealth exceeds minimum threshold
6. **Complete Hawl** - Wealth held for one full Islamic year

## What is Nisab?

**Nisab** is the minimum threshold of wealth that triggers Zakat obligation.

### Historical Standard
Traditionally, Nisab is calculated based on:
- **Gold**: 87.48 grams (2.75 troy ounces)
- **Silver**: 612.36 grams (19.75 troy ounces)

### Modern Equivalents (2026)
- **Gold Nisab**: ~$5,000 - $6,000 USD
- **Silver Nisab**: ~$300 - $400 USD

Most scholars today use the **gold standard** as it's more practical.

## Types of Wealth Subject to Zakat

### 1. Cash & Currency
- Physical money
- Bank deposits
- Savings accounts
- Cryptocurrency (per some scholars)

### 2. Gold & Silver
- Jewelry (if hoarded for value)
- Bullion or coins
- Ornaments kept for storage

### 3. Business Inventory
- Stock intended for sale
- Trade goods
- Commercial merchandise

### 4. Investments
- Stocks and bonds
- Mutual funds
- Investment accounts

### 5. Rental Income
- Annual rental income
- Commercial property income
- Investment property returns

### 6. Agricultural Produce
- Grain crops (wheat, barley, dates)
- Different rate: 5-10% (not 2.5%)

## NOT Subject to Zakat

❌ Primary residence (home you live in)  
❌ Personal vehicles (car you use)  
❌ Clothing and personal items  
❌ Household furniture and utensils  
❌ Tools of trade (for many schools)  
❌ Jewelry worn regularly  

## Zakat Rate

### Standard Rate: 2.5%
Formula: **Zakatable Wealth × 0.025 = Zakat Due**

This represents **1/40** of wealth per Islamic jurisprudence.

### Example
- **Total Wealth**: $40,000
- **Zakat Rate**: 2.5%
- **Zakat Due**: $40,000 × 0.025 = **$1,000**

### Agricultural Exceptions
- **Rain-fed crops**: 10% (no irrigation needed)
- **Irrigated crops**: 5% (artificial irrigation used)

## When is Zakat Due?

### Zakat al-Mal (Wealth Zakat)
- **Frequency**: Once per year
- **Duration**: 1 complete Islamic (lunar) or Gregorian year
- **Timing**: 
  - Can choose Islamic calendar (354 days)
  - Or Gregorian calendar (365 days)
  - Or based on personal Zakat anniversary
- **When to Pay**: Before or on Zakat year anniversary

### Zakat al-Fitr (Charity at Eid)
- **Timing**: Before Eid al-Fitr prayer
- **Amount**: Fixed per person (not percentage)
- **Typical Amount**: $3-10 USD per person
- **Who Pays**: Head of household for dependents
- **Purpose**: Purification before Eid celebration

## Calculation Process

### Step 1: List All Assets
```
Cash/Savings:           $15,000
Gold (50g × $65):       $3,250
Stocks:                 $10,000
Business Inventory:     $8,000
Rental Income (annual): $5,000
------------------------
Total Assets:           $41,250
```

### Step 2: Subtract Liabilities
```
Personal Debts:         -$3,000
Business Loan:          -$2,000
------------------------
Total Liabilities:      -$5,000
```

### Step 3: Calculate Net Wealth
```
Net Wealth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
Net Wealth = $41,250 - $5,000 = $36,250
```

### Step 4: Check Nisab
```
Nisab Threshold:        $5,668 (87.48g gold)
Your Net Wealth:        $36,250
Status:                 ✓ ABOVE NISAB
Obligation:             YES - Zakat is due
```

### Step 5: Calculate Zakat
```
Zakat Due = Net Wealth × 2.5%
Zakat Due = $36,250 × 0.025 = $906.25
```

## Recipients of Zakat

According to Qur'an 9:60, Zakat recipients are:

1. **The Poor** (Al-Fuqaraa)
   - Those with no means of survival
   - Cannot meet basic needs

2. **The Needy** (Al-Masakeen)
   - Those with insufficient means
   - Can partially meet needs

3. **Zakat Collectors** (Amelin-alaiha)
   - Those employed to distribute Zakat
   - Administrator salaries

4. **Those Whose Hearts are Won** (Muallafatu QulubUhum)
   - New converts to Islam
   - Those helping Islamic cause

5. **Freeing Slaves/Debtors** (Fir-Riqab)
   - Ransom for enslaved Muslims
   - Paying off debts

6. **Those in Debt** (Al-Gharimeen)
   - Those with unmanageable debts
   - Seeking legitimate repairs of finances

7. **In Allah's Cause** (Fi Sabilillah)
   - Islamic education and da'wah
   - Jihad (struggle) in God's path

8. **Travelers/Wayfarers** (Ibn-as-Sabeel)
   - Those stranded away from home
   - Travelers in need of assistance

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Mistake 1**: Calculating Zakat before one year passes  
✓ **Solution**: Wait for complete Islamic/Gregorian year

❌ **Mistake 2**: Not deducting debts and liabilities  
✓ **Solution**: Always subtract legitimate debts first

❌ **Mistake 3**: Including non-zakatable items  
✓ **Solution**: Exclude primary residence, personal use items

❌ **Mistake 4**: Applying wrong rate to agricultural income  
✓ **Solution**: Use 5-10% for crops, 2.5% for other wealth

❌ **Mistake 5**: Delaying payment after year ends  
✓ **Solution**: Pay on or before anniversary date

## Different Schools of Thought

Islamic jurisprudence has 4 main schools, with variations on Zakat:

### Hanafi School
- More stringent on asset types
- Generally strict interpretation

### Maliki School
- Multiple interpretations for certain assets
- Flexible on some renewable resources

### Shafi'i School
- Detailed rules on each asset type
- Very comprehensive approach

### Hanbali School
- Intermediate positions
- Practical approach

**Note**: This app uses standard Islamic principles. Consult your local Islamic scholar for school-specific rulings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I pay Zakat early?**  
A: Some scholars allow payment 1 month early with proper intention. Better to consult your scholar.

**Q: Can I give Zakat to non-Muslims?**  
A: Traditional ruling: Only to Muslims. Consult your scholar for interpretation.

**Q: What if I'm below Nisab?**  
A: Zakat is not obligatory, but voluntary charity (Sadaqah) is always encouraged.

**Q: Can I give Zakat to family?**  
A: Depends on school of thought. It's allowed in some schools but not others. Ask your scholar.

**Q: What about Zakat on cryptocurrency?**  
A: Modern scholars increasingly recognize crypto as zakatable. Treat like other investments (2.5%).
